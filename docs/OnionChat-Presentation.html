The comfortable lie

"But it's end-to-end encrypted ‚Ä¶"

‚úď What encryption protects

The content of a message, while it travels between two phones.

‚úó What it does NOT protect

  • Who you are (phone number, IP)
  • Who you talk to and when (metadata)
  • A company server that can be subpoenaed
  • A compromised phone that reads the screen

Encryption secures the letter ‚ÄĒ not the envelope, the post office, or the reader looking over your shoulder.

Case 1 ¬∑ Telegram

"Encrypted" ‚ÄĒ yet it hands your IP and phone number to police

  • Telegram chats are not end-to-end encrypted by default ‚ÄĒ only opt-in "Secret Chats" are.
  • Everything else lives on Telegram's servers, which hold the keys.
  • September 2024: after its CEO's arrest, Telegram's policy now shares users' IP address and phone number with authorities on valid legal requests.
IP + ‚ėé

Two data points that turn an "anonymous" account into a name and a home address.

Case 2 ¬∑ WhatsApp & Pegasus

Perfect encryption is useless when the phone itself is hacked

  • WhatsApp encrypts content end-to-end (the Signal protocol) ‚ÄĒ genuinely good.
  • But Pegasus (NSO Group) is zero-click spyware that takes over the phone's OS and reads messages after they're decrypted ‚ÄĒ plus mic, camera, screen.
  • In 2019 it was delivered through a single WhatsApp call. The encryption was never broken ‚ÄĒ it was bypassed.

ūüĒď The lesson

If the endpoint or the company is the weak point, end-to-end encryption alone doesn't save you.

The pattern

Three weaknesses every mainstream messenger shares

1

Identity = a phone number

Tied to your real name, SIM, and IP. Registration is deanonymisation.

2

Encryption fixes none of these. So the architecture has to change.

How OnionChat works

Identity ¬∑ Encryption ¬∑ Transport

  • Identity ‚ÄĒ an Ed25519 key ‚Üí a Tor v3 .onion. The address is the identity; verifying a signature against it proves the sender. No PKI.
  • Encryption ‚ÄĒ every message is a sealed box: fresh X25519 key exchange ‚Üí AES-256-GCM, then signed. Confidential, authentic, forward-secret.
  • Transport ‚ÄĒ direct P2P: each phone hosts its own onion and dials the contact. Optional untrusted relay adds offline delivery ‚ÄĒ it only ever sees ciphertext.
  • Ephemeral ‚ÄĒ conversations never touch disk.
